Wedding Website Design Document

1. Project Overview

A fully custom, highly interactive wedding website to serve as the digital hub for the upcoming wedding on March 13, 2027. The platform will handle RSVPs, provide venue/travel information, link registries, and act as a secure, verified portal for 70 expected guests.

2. Event Details

Date: March 13, 2027

Time: 3:00 PM

Venue: The River Road and Jasmine Houses and Gardens

Location: Irmo, SC

Vibe: Intimate, Southern Botanical, Turn-of-the-Century Charm, Romantic Gardens.

3. Aesthetic & UI/UX Design

The visual language will draw heavily from the venue's natural beauty and a "Coastal Carolina Sunrise" theme.

Color Palette (Modular & Themed):

Primary: Sage Green (soft, natural)

Secondary: Palmetto Green (deep, rich foliage)

Accents: Splash of Sunrise Pink (soft pastel) and a hint of Sunrise Orange (warm, inviting glow).

Neutrals: Warm off-whites (creams, linen colors) to give it an elegant, physical-card feel rather than harsh, digital white.

Typography:

Headings: A classic, elegant Serif font (e.g., Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond) to reflect the vintage venue.

Body: A clean, highly readable Sans-Serif font (e.g., Lato or Montserrat).

Imagery: Subtle background motifs of Magnolia leaves, Palmetto trees, and botanical sketches. Engagement photos will be featured prominently with soft, rounded corners and drop shadows.

NEEDS TO WORK WELL ON BOTH DESKTOP AND MOBILE!!

4. Core Features (MVP)

Landing Page: Beautiful hero image (engagement photo), countdown timer to March 13, and the general date/location.

Our Story: A timeline or short narrative of the relationship, complete with a photo gallery.

Guest Verification / Security Wall: A gateway where guests enter their name/email (or a secret code sent via Evite) to gain access to sensitive information.

Information Hub (Protected):

Schedule: Exact timelines for the ceremony and reception.

Travel & Lodging: Hotel blocks, Airbnb recommendations, and parking info.

Columbia/Irmo Guide: "Make a weekend of it" – favorite restaurants, sights, and things to do.

FAQs: Dress code, plus-ones, dietary restrictions.

RSVP System (Protected): Digital form connected to Firestore. Guests can confirm attendance, select meals (if applicable), and list dietary restrictions.

Registry/Payment: Links to external registries (Zola, Target, etc.) or integration with a payment processor (Stripe) for a "Honeymoon Fund".

Admin Portal (Hidden): Dashboard for the couple to view RSVP lists, edit FAQs, approve users, and manage content.

5. Future / Stretch Features (Post-MVP)

Digital Evite Integration: Generate unique, secure links to send out via email/text. Clicking the link auto-verifies the guest.

Interactive Social Wall: A feed where verified guests can post comments, well-wishes, and upload photos before, during, and after the wedding.

Live Photo Gallery: Integration with Firebase Storage where guests can upload their candid shots from the reception.

6. Technical Architecture

Frontend: React.js (Component-based UI)

Build Tool: Vite.js (Fast HMR, optimized builds)

Styling: Tailwind CSS (Utility-first, responsive, highly modular theme config)

Backend / Database: Firebase (Authentication, Firestore for RSVPs/Posts, Storage for Photos)

Hosting: Firebase Hosting or Vercel
